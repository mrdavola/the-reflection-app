## Design Context

### Users

Refleckt is used by two audiences in two different physical contexts.

**Teachers** are tired, time-poor, and reading the screen during prep periods, between classes, or at the end of a long day. They need to triage 21 reflections in under five minutes and decide who to check on tomorrow. The interface lives in bright classrooms on Chromebooks, projected to whole classes, and on personal laptops at the kitchen table.

**Students** (often under 14) arrive at the app via a share link, with no login. Many are anxious about reflecting — about being judged, about saying the wrong thing, about typing in a second language. The interface needs to feel like a kind, patient prompt, not a test or a grade book.

### Brand Personality

Three words: **warm, calm, classroom-ready**.

Refleckt is the thoughtful colleague who hands you the right notebook at the right moment — never the gleaming dashboard with eight charts. The voice is grounded, generous, and treats reflection as a craft worth taking seriously. It avoids the cheerful condescension common in K-12 software and the cold precision of analytics tools.

Emotional goals: confidence (for the teacher), safety (for the student), focus (for both).

### Aesthetic Direction

**Marginalia.** A teacher's lesson-planning notebook — warm paper, considered ink, the occasional underlined word, restraint everywhere else. Editorial, not "EdTech."

- **Theme:** light only, optimized for daytime classrooms (bright rooms, glare on Chromebook screens).
- **Surface:** warm ivory backgrounds (never pure white), printed-ink charcoal foreground (never pure black).
- **Typography:** Petrona (display, warm humanist serif) paired with Atkinson Hyperlegible (body, designed for low-vision readers, handles Spanish accents cleanly).
- **Color:** a single deep ink-blue primary used sparingly. A muted carmine accent reserved for emphasis, like a teacher's red pen marginalia. Triage colors (sunny / orange / dusty blue) preserved but tuned to fit the warm palette.
- **Motion:** restrained. No bouncy springs. Slow ease-out for entrances; instant feedback for interaction.
- **Decoration:** subtle. Underline emphasis instead of bold. Ruled-line dividers that suggest composition paper. No gradients, no glassmorphism, no glow halos, no purple-to-blue everything.

**Anti-references:** generic edtech (Khan Academy purple, ClassDojo cartoon), B2B SaaS dashboards (Linear, Stripe homepage gradients), AI-product aesthetic (cyan-on-dark, neon accents, gradient text).

### Design Principles

1. **Paper before pixels.** Surfaces are warm and tactile, never glassy. If a treatment would not be possible with ink and paper, justify it.
2. **Restraint reads as care.** A single underlined word does more than three colored badges. Hierarchy lives in type weight, size, and space — not in fills and gradients.
3. **Bilingual by construction.** Layouts must accommodate Spanish text expansion (~20–30% longer) without truncating, wrapping awkwardly, or breaking the grid.
4. **Legibility over fashion.** This is education software. Atkinson Hyperlegible body, generous line-height, line lengths capped at 65–75ch. Contrast meets WCAG AA at minimum.
5. **Triage at a glance.** The teacher's eye should land on the right student in two seconds. Status colors are semantic and consistent across every screen.
