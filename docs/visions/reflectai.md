# ReflectAI: Product Vision Document
## A Reflection-Forward AI Platform Built on Visible Thinking Routines

---

## The One-Sentence Pitch

MagicSchool made AI accessible for teachers doing everyday tasks. ReflectAI does the same thing but for the hardest, most important part of learning: making student thinking visible, structured, and actionable.

---

## Why This Exists

Every teacher knows reflection matters. Robert Marzano's research shows that when students talk about what they're learning, they learn twice. Harvard's Project Zero has spent decades proving that thinking routines make invisible cognitive processes visible and teachable. The Portrait of a Graduate frameworks across the country demand metacognition, critical thinking, and self-direction.

But here's the gap: teachers don't have time to facilitate deep, structured reflection with every student every day. Written exit tickets get surface-level responses. Class discussions only reach the confident kids. Journaling buries insights in notebooks no one reads. And the quiet kid in the back? Their thinking stays invisible.

ReflectAI closes this gap. It takes the proven pedagogical framework of visible thinking routines, wraps them in AI that adapts in real time to what students actually say, and delivers the insights back to teachers in a dashboard they can act on before the bell rings. Students talk. AI listens, structures, and analyzes. Teachers see the thinking of every kid in the room, not just the loud ones.

Start Human. Use AI. End Human.

The teacher picks the routine. The student does the thinking. The AI makes that thinking visible. The teacher closes the loop.

---

## Core Philosophy

### This Is Not a Chatbot for Kids
Students don't "chat with AI." They engage in structured thinking routines that have decades of research behind them. The AI is invisible scaffolding, not a conversation partner. It generates follow-up prompts within the structure of the routine, analyzes the depth of thinking, and surfaces patterns. But the student experience feels like a guided reflection, not a tech demo.

### Reflection Is the Product, Not a Feature
Most edtech platforms bolt on reflection as an afterthought. A text box at the end. A rating scale. ReflectAI is reflection all the way down. Every screen, every interaction, every piece of data is in service of making thinking visible and actionable.

### Voice First, Writing Welcome
Young students, ELL students, and students who struggle with written expression often have rich thinking trapped behind a writing barrier. Voice removes that barrier. Students speak their thinking, and the AI transcribes, analyzes, and responds. Text input is always available, but voice is the default path.

### The Teacher Is the Expert
AI generates prompts, analyzes responses, and suggests next steps. But the teacher decides what matters. The dashboard is designed to amplify teacher judgment, not replace it. Every AI insight includes enough context for the teacher to agree, disagree, or dig deeper.

## The Thinking Routine Library

This is the backbone. Instead of generic "reflect on your learning" prompts, every reflection is grounded in a researched thinking routine. The AI knows the structure of each routine and generates adaptive prompts within that structure.

### Tier 1: Core Routines (Ship at Launch)

**See Think Wonder**
- Structure: What do you see? What do you think about that? What does it make you wonder?
- Best for: Observation, curiosity activation, stimulus-based learning
- AI behavior: After "See," the AI probes for specificity. After "Think," it pushes for reasoning. After "Wonder," it celebrates genuine curiosity and suggests where that question could lead.
- Teacher setup: Upload or link a stimulus (image, video, text, artifact, equation, map, dataset)

**I Used to Think... Now I Think...**
- Structure: What did you used to think about [topic]? What do you think now? What changed your mind?
- Best for: End of unit, after new information, conceptual shifts
- AI behavior: Looks for genuine cognitive shift vs. surface-level restating. Flags when "now I think" is just a rephrasing of "I used to think" rather than a real change.
- Teacher setup: Identify the concept or topic

**Think Pair Share (Solo Mode)**
- Structure: Think (record initial thinking) → (pair step happens live in class, not in app) → Share (record refined thinking after pair discussion)
- Best for: Building on peer dialogue, pre/post comparison
- AI behavior: Compares the Think recording to the Share recording and identifies what shifted, deepened, or was added through the peer conversation
- Teacher setup: Set the prompt, set a timer for the pair step

**Connect Extend Challenge**
- Structure: How does this connect to something you already know? How does this extend your thinking? What is still challenging or confusing?
- Best for: Processing new information, making connections
- AI behavior: Validates connections (are they substantive or surface?), probes extensions for depth, normalizes and celebrates the challenge acknowledgment
- Teacher setup: Identify the new content or experience to reflect on

**Claim Support Question**
- Structure: Make a claim about the topic. What evidence supports your claim? What questions remain?
- Best for: Argumentation, evidence-based thinking, critical analysis
- AI behavior: Evaluates whether the "support" actually supports the "claim" or is tangential. Flags unsupported claims gently. Celebrates strong evidence reasoning.
- Teacher setup: Provide the topic or text to analyze

**What Makes You Say That?**
- Structure: State an observation or interpretation. Explain your reasoning.
- Best for: Pushing beyond surface answers, building reasoning habits
- AI behavior: This is a single-prompt routine that the AI uses as a follow-up pattern everywhere. Any time a student makes a claim, the AI's next prompt is essentially "What makes you say that?" adapted to context.
- Teacher setup: Can be standalone or embedded as a follow-up mode in other routines

**Headlines**
- Structure: If you had to write a headline that captures the most important aspect of [topic/lesson/experience], what would it be? Why?
- Best for: Synthesis, identifying main ideas, prioritizing information
- AI behavior: Analyzes whether the headline captures a central idea or a peripheral detail. Asks follow-up about why that particular angle matters.
- Teacher setup: Identify what students are summarizing

**3-2-1 Bridge**
- Structure: Before the lesson, give 3 thoughts, 2 questions, 1 analogy. After the lesson, repeat. Then bridge: how has your thinking shifted?
- Best for: Pre/post learning comparison, metacognition
- AI behavior: Explicitly compares before and after responses. Maps the bridge between initial and evolved thinking. Highlights growth.
- Teacher setup: Run part 1 before the lesson, part 2 after. App links them automatically.

### Tier 2: Advanced Routines (Post-Launch)

**Circle of Viewpoints**
- Structure: Pick a perspective different from your own. What might that person think? Why? How does considering this change your own view?
- Best for: Empathy, perspective-taking, controversial or multi-sided topics

**Compass Points**
- Structure: E (excited about), W (worrying about), N (need to know), S (stance or suggestion)
- Best for: Decision-making, project planning, evaluating proposals

**Color Symbol Image**
- Structure: Choose a color that represents your understanding. Choose a symbol. Choose an image. Explain each.
- Best for: Metaphorical thinking, creative expression, arts integration

**Tug of War**
- Structure: Identify factors that pull toward one side of a dilemma and factors that pull toward the other
- Best for: Exploring tensions, ethical dilemmas, debate preparation

**Generate Sort Connect Elaborate**
- Structure: Generate ideas → Sort into categories → Connect categories → Elaborate on connections
- Best for: Concept mapping, brainstorming, organizing complex information

**The 4 C's**
- Structure: Connections, Challenges, Concepts, Changes
- Best for: Text-based discussion, reading response, content processing

**Micro-Lab Protocol**
- Structure: Timed rounds of speaking and listening with structured response
- Best for: Equitable discussion, active listening practice

### Custom Routines
- Teachers can build their own routines by defining:
  - Number of steps (1-5)
  - Prompt text for each step
  - Whether AI generates adaptive follow-ups between steps
  - Whether responses are voice, text, or choice (drawing/image upload for younger students)
- Custom routines can be saved to a personal library and shared with other teachers

## Student Experience (Screen by Screen)

### Entry: Zero Friction

**Option A: QR Code Scan**
- Teacher projects a QR code on the board
- Student scans with any device (phone, tablet, Chromebook camera)
- Opens directly to the reflection, no app install needed
- Student enters first name (and optional last initial)
- Taps "Join" and they're in

**Option B: Join Link**
- Teacher shares a short link (e.g., reflectai.app/join/abc123)
- Student clicks, enters name, they're in
- Link can be posted in Google Classroom, Padlet, LMS, email, wherever

**Option C: Google Classroom Integration**
- Teacher connects their Google Classroom roster
- When students click the link, they're auto-identified by their Google account
- No name entry needed, data automatically mapped to the right student
- Teacher sees real names in the dashboard instead of self-entered names

**Option D: Class Code**
- Teacher gives a short alphanumeric code (like Kahoot, Gimkit, etc.)
- Student goes to reflectai.app, enters the code, enters name, they're in

**All options require**: microphone permission (camera optional, configurable by teacher)

### The Reflection Flow

**Screen 1: Welcome + Context**
- Clean, calming screen. Not gamified, not busy.
- Shows: the thinking routine name, the teacher's prompt or stimulus (if any), and a brief plain-language explanation of what they'll do
- Example: "See Think Wonder: Look at the image your teacher shared. You'll tell us what you see, what you think about it, and what it makes you wonder."
- Big "Begin" button
- If the teacher uploaded a stimulus (image, text, video, link), it's displayed here and remains accessible throughout

**Screen 2-N: Routine Steps (One Per Screen)**
- Each step of the thinking routine gets its own screen
- The step label is clear: "Step 1: What do you SEE?" / "Step 2: What do you THINK?" / "Step 3: What do you WONDER?"
- Large record button (pulsing, inviting)
- 15-second minimum before "Next" appears (configurable by teacher for different grade bands)
- Real-time transcription appears as they speak (optional, can be hidden for younger students who might get distracted reading their own words)
- Text input toggle: student can switch to typing if they prefer
- Stimulus remains visible in a collapsible panel if the teacher provided one
- Timer visible if the teacher set time limits

**Between Steps: AI Adaptive Follow-Up (Optional)**
- If enabled by the teacher, the AI generates one follow-up question between steps based on what the student just said
- This happens within the structure of the routine, not as a random tangent
- Example: Student says "I see a lot of people standing in a line." AI follow-up: "You noticed people in a line. Can you describe what else you see around them, like where they are or what they might be waiting for?"
- This deepens responses without breaking the routine's flow
- If disabled, students move straight to the next step

**Final Screen: Your Thinking Snapshot**
- After completing all steps, the student sees their results:
  - **Thinking Depth Score**: a simple visual (not a grade, think more like a depth meter: surface → developing → deep → transfer)
  - **Your Strongest Move**: which part of the routine showed the most depth (e.g., "Your 'Wonder' question showed real curiosity")
  - **A Nudge**: one specific suggestion for deepening their thinking next time (e.g., "Next time, try connecting your observation to something you already know")
  - **Growth Over Time**: if they've done this routine before, a simple visual showing their trajectory
- Tone is ALWAYS encouraging, specific, and forward-looking
- For younger students (K-2), this simplifies to an emoji-scale depth indicator with one sentence of feedback

### Student Portfolio View (Persistent)
- If connected via Google Classroom or logged-in account, students accumulate a portfolio
- Shows all their reflections across routines and subjects
- Simple growth visualizations: "You've done 12 reflections this month" with a depth-over-time sparkline
- Can revisit past reflections, listen to recordings, read transcripts
- Exportable as a PDF portfolio or shareable link

## Teacher Experience (Screen by Screen)

### Setup: 60 Seconds to Launch

**Step 1: Pick a Routine**
- Visual card grid showing all available thinking routines
- Each card has: routine name, 1-sentence description, best-for tags (e.g., "observation," "synthesis," "pre/post"), grade band recommendation
- Tap to select
- "Custom" card at the end for building your own

**Step 2: Add Context (Optional but Recommended)**
- **Learning target / standard**: text field or voice recording. AI uses this to calibrate analysis and generate adaptive follow-ups aligned to the objective.
- **Stimulus**: upload an image, paste a link (YouTube video, article, etc.), type a text passage, or just describe what students will be reflecting on. The stimulus appears on students' screens during the reflection.
- **Grade band**: auto-set from class roster if using Google Classroom, otherwise manually selected. This calibrates: language complexity of AI prompts, depth expectations for scoring, vocabulary in feedback.

**Step 3: Configure (Expandable, Sensible Defaults)**
- **AI follow-ups**: on/off toggle (default: on). When on, AI generates one adaptive follow-up question between routine steps.
- **Voice minimum**: slider for minimum speaking time per step (default: 15 seconds for grades 3+, 10 seconds for K-2)
- **Language**: primary language for prompts and feedback (English, Spanish, or auto-detect)
- **Response mode**: voice only, text only, or student's choice (default: student's choice)
- **Time limit per step**: optional, useful for keeping the whole class moving together
- **Show transcription to students**: on/off (default: on for grades 3+, off for K-2)
- **Student results visibility**: full snapshot / simplified / none (default: full for grades 3+, simplified for K-2)

**Step 4: Share**
- Generates simultaneously:
  - A QR code (downloadable as image, projectable)
  - A short join link (copyable)
  - A class code (4-6 characters, speakable)
  - A Google Classroom post (if connected, one-click)
- Teacher projects the QR code or shares the link, and students are in within seconds

**Total setup time**: 30-60 seconds for a routine with defaults. 2-3 minutes if customizing everything.

### The Live Monitoring Dashboard

This is where the magic happens. While students are reflecting, the teacher sees a real-time dashboard.

**Layout: Split View**

**Left Panel: Class Pulse (Live)**
- Shows how many students have joined, how many are reflecting, how many are done
- Progress bar: "14 of 22 students have completed their reflection"
- Students appear as bubbles/avatars that move from "Joined" → "Reflecting" → "Done" in real time
- If a student gets flagged (content alert, negative tone), their bubble turns red with a subtle alert icon

**Right Panel: Insights Stream (Builds as Students Finish)**
As students complete their reflections, insights populate in real time:

**Class Thinking Map** (the headline feature)
- A visual representation of the class's collective thinking organized by the routine's structure
- For See Think Wonder: three columns showing clustered themes from what students saw, thought, and wondered
- AI groups similar responses together and labels the clusters
- Example: 12 students noticed "people standing in a line," 8 noticed "a sign in the background," 3 noticed "the weather looks cold"
- This gives the teacher an instant read on where the class's attention went and what they missed
- Teacher can tap any cluster to see which students contributed to it and read/listen to their specific responses

**Priority Cards**
- Individual student cards that only surface when something is notable:
  - **Depth alert (positive)**: "Maya made a cross-curricular connection between the water cycle and the drainage system she saw in the image"
  - **Depth alert (concern)**: "Jordan's responses across all three steps were single sentences with no elaboration"
  - **Mindset flag**: "Alex's tone is notably negative today compared to their last 3 reflections"
  - **Content alert**: "Sam mentioned feeling unsafe. Immediate review recommended." (with direct link to transcript)
- Cards are color-coded:
  - Green/teal: notable depth or connection (celebrate)
  - Amber: low depth or engagement (check in)
  - Red: content safety alert (act now)

**Class Summary** (generates after ~75% of students finish)
- Two-paragraph AI-generated summary:
  - **Paragraph 1 — What They're Thinking**: Themes, patterns, misconceptions, surprises across the class
  - **Paragraph 2 — What You Could Do Next**: Specific, actionable instructional moves based on what the class demonstrated. Not generic advice, but moves tied to what THIS class said about THIS content.
- Example: "Most students connected the image to colonialism but focused on the visual elements (clothing, architecture) rather than power dynamics. Consider using a Circle of Viewpoints routine next to push them beyond observation into interpretation. You might ask: 'What would someone who lived in this building think about the people in the line?'"

**Individual Student View** (tap any student name or card)
- Full transcript of their reflection, step by step
- Audio/video playback
- AI analysis:
  - Thinking depth per step (surface / developing / deep / transfer)
  - Overall routine score (1-4)
  - Mindset: growth / neutral / fixed
  - Tone: engaged / neutral / disengaged / concerned
  - Key quotes extracted
  - Cross-curricular connections detected
  - Comparison to their previous reflections (if available)
- What the student saw as their feedback
- Teacher can add a personal note that gets sent to the student

### Post-Session Analytics

**Class Trends Dashboard**
- After multiple reflection sessions, the teacher sees trends:
  - Class-wide thinking depth over time (are they getting deeper?)
  - Most common routine step where depth drops off (e.g., "Your students consistently give deep 'See' responses but shallow 'Wonder' responses")
  - Mindset and tone trends over weeks/months
  - Individual student growth trajectories
  - Which routines produce the deepest thinking for this class

**Exportable Reports**
- PDF summary of a reflection session (for admin observations, PLCs, portfolio)
- CSV export of student data (for gradebook integration or further analysis)
- Shareable link to class thinking map (for co-teachers, coaches, or PLCs)

## AI Behavior: Deep Spec

### Thinking Depth Scoring Model

Rather than a generic "understanding score," the AI evaluates thinking depth on a 4-level rubric aligned to visible thinking research:

| Level | Label | What It Looks Like |
|-------|-------|-------------------|
| 1 | Surface | Restates the prompt, gives one-word answers, describes without interpreting |
| 2 | Developing | Shows some reasoning, makes a basic connection, begins to explain "why" |
| 3 | Deep | Connects across concepts, provides evidence for claims, questions assumptions, shows genuine curiosity |
| 4 | Transfer | Applies thinking to new contexts, makes cross-curricular connections, generates original questions that could drive further inquiry |

The AI scores each step of the routine independently AND gives an overall depth score. This lets the teacher see exactly where in the routine the thinking went shallow.

### Adaptive Follow-Up Generation Rules

The AI generates follow-up questions that stay within the routine's pedagogical structure:

1. **Never break the routine**: If a student is on the "Think" step of See Think Wonder, the follow-up must probe their thinking, not redirect to wondering or seeing
2. **Reference specifics**: Always reference what the student actually said, never generate generic probes
3. **Push one level deeper**: If the student gave a Surface response, the follow-up should scaffold toward Developing. If they gave Deep, push toward Transfer
4. **Respect the 15-second minimum**: Follow-ups should be open enough that students need to think and speak for more than a few seconds
5. **Match developmental level**: K-2 follow-ups use simple language and concrete references. High school follow-ups use academic vocabulary and abstract reasoning prompts
6. **If off-topic**: Gently redirect back to the routine step without shaming (e.g., "That's interesting! Let's bring it back to what you saw in the image. What else did you notice?")

### Feedback Tone Rules

All student-facing feedback follows these rules:

1. **Celebrate specifics, not effort alone**: "Your connection between the water cycle and the drainage system shows transfer thinking" is better than "Great job!"
2. **One nudge per reflection**: Don't overwhelm with multiple improvement suggestions. Pick the one move that would make the biggest difference next time.
3. **Frame growth, not deficit**: "Next time, try..." not "You didn't..."
4. **Normalize the Challenge step**: When students say something is confusing or hard in routines like Connect Extend Challenge, the feedback explicitly celebrates this as strong metacognition ("Knowing what you don't know yet is one of the most powerful thinking moves there is")
5. **Never harsh, never hollow**: Feedback should feel like a warm, specific, knowledgeable teacher who noticed exactly what you said

### Content Safety System

- Real-time keyword scanning during transcription for: self-harm, suicide, abuse, violence, bullying, weapon references
- Immediate teacher alert (in-dashboard notification + email) when triggered
- False positive design: teacher can review transcript and recording to verify before taking action
- Profanity detection with teacher review (auto-flagged, not auto-punished)
- Sensitive topic handling: when a student's reflection touches on personal difficulty within an academic context (e.g., project about mental health), the system doesn't flag unless there are indicators of personal crisis

### Language Support

- Primary UI and AI prompts in English and Spanish at launch
- Auto-detect: if a student responds in a different language, the AI adapts its follow-ups and feedback to that language
- Teacher sees translated transcript alongside original language
- Thinking routine step labels available in both languages
- Stretch goal: real-time translation of any response into teacher's preferred language

## Suggested Tech Stack

- **Frontend**: Next.js + React (responsive, works on all devices including Chromebooks)
- **Styling**: Tailwind CSS with a custom design system (calming, education-forward, not gamified)
- **Database**: Firebase Firestore (real-time updates for the live dashboard)
- **Auth**: Firebase Auth (Google OAuth for teachers, anonymous/name-only for students) + Google Classroom API for roster sync
- **AI**: Gemini API (prompt generation, analysis, feedback, class summaries)
- **Audio/Video**: Browser MediaRecorder API for capture, Firebase Storage for files
- **Transcription**: Whisper API or Google Speech-to-Text (needs to handle young children's speech patterns)
- **QR Generation**: Client-side QR code library (qrcode.js or similar)
- **Hosting**: Vercel
- **Real-time**: Firebase Realtime Database or Firestore listeners for live dashboard updates

## Design Direction

### Not This
- Gamified (no points, leaderboards, or competitive mechanics, thinking is not a game)
- Busy or overwhelming (teachers are already overstimulated)
- Dark mode default (this is used in bright classrooms on projectors)
- Childish illustrations (this needs to work K-12 without being condescending to older students)

### This
- **Calm and focused**: think Notion meets Headspace. Clean space that says "this is where thinking happens"
- **Warm but professional**: works for a 2nd grade classroom AND a high school AP class
- **Light mode primary** with high contrast for projector visibility
- **Typography-forward**: beautiful, readable type that makes student words feel valued
- **Generous whitespace**: the student's thinking is the content, the UI stays out of the way
- **Subtle depth cues**: soft shadows, gentle color gradients for depth indicators, no harsh borders
- **Color palette**: a primary blue-teal (thinking, clarity, calm) with warm accent (amber or coral for alerts and celebrations). Green for growth indicators. Neutral grays for structure.
- **Motion**: gentle transitions between routine steps (the experience should feel like turning a page, not clicking a button). Loading states feel meditative, not anxious.
- **Student-facing screens**: extra large type, minimal UI, one action per screen. A 6-year-old should be able to navigate this without reading instructions.
- **Teacher dashboard**: information-dense but organized. Think Bloomberg Terminal for student thinking, but beautiful.

## MVP Scope (Build This First)

### Phase 1: Core Loop
1. Teacher picks See Think Wonder (one routine, hardcoded)
2. Teacher optionally adds a learning target and stimulus image
3. Teacher gets a QR code and join link
4. Students scan/click, enter name, and do the 3-step reflection via voice
5. AI transcribes, analyzes, and generates feedback
6. Students see their thinking snapshot
7. Teacher sees a live dashboard with class thinking map and student cards

### Phase 2: Routine Library
8. Add the full Tier 1 routine library (8 routines)
9. Custom routine builder
10. Google Classroom integration for roster sync

### Phase 3: Growth & Trends
11. Student portfolio view with growth over time
12. Teacher trend analytics across sessions
13. Exportable reports (PDF, CSV)
14. Shared routine library (teachers share custom routines)

### Phase 4: Advanced
15. Tier 2 routines
16. Multi-language support
17. Admin/coach view across multiple teachers
18. API for LMS integration
